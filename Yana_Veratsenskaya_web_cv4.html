Boeing 747

The Boeing 747, known as the "Queen of the Skies," is a large, long-range, wide-body jet airliner developed by Boeing. It features a distinctive upper deck hump and four powerful engines, allowing it to cover long intercontinental routes. The 747 can typically seat between 400 and 600 passengers, depending on the configuration. Over its many variants, from the 747-100 to the 747-8, it has set new standards in both passenger travel and cargo transport, becoming an iconic aircraft in aviation history.

Specifications

  • Length: 71 meter
  • Amount of seats: 550
  • Total weight: 400,000 kg

Interesting Facts

  • The Boeing 747 was the first wide-body airplane ever produced.
  • It was initially designed for both passenger and cargo transport.
  • The 747 can reach a cruising speed of 0.85 Mach (about 913 km/h).
  • The upper deck of the 747 was originally designed as a lounge area.
